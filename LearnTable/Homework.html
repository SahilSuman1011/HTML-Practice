Linus Torvalds

The Creator of Linux

Biography

Linus Torvalds is a Finnish software engineer who is the creator and, historically, the lead developer of the Linux kernel, used by Linux distributions and other operating systems such as Android. He also created the distributed version control system Git.

Achievements

  • 1991 - IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award
  • 2014 - IEEE Computer Pioneer Award
  • 2012 - Internet Hall of Fame
  • 2010 - C&C Prize
  • 2008 - Hall of Fellows
  • 2005 - Vollum Award
  • 2003 - Linus (Moon)
  • 2001 - Takeda Award
  • 2000 - Lovelace Medal
  • 1998 - EFF Pioneer Award
